Residential — Chalakudy, Kerala
The Project

A 4,000 sqft sanctuary where Kerala tradition meets contemporary restraint.

Pitched terracotta roofs, timber-slatted ceilings, polished stone floors — the language is Keralan. The proportions, the light, the way interior and exterior dissolve into each other — that is ours. A house designed to feel as though it has always been there.

Location Chalakudy, Kerala
Typology Residential — 4,000 sqft
Scope Architecture, Interiors, CGI
Status In Design
The Courtyard

Stone, timber, and open sky.

The plan organises around a central courtyard — a gravel garden with a single boulder and stepping stones. Every room opens onto it. The courtyard is not decoration. It is the heart of the house — the space that cools, connects, and centres.

The Principle

Traditional is not a style. It is a way of building that already knows the climate, the light, and the land.
